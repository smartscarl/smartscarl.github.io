How the test works

Choose a sample, then type it as quickly and accurately as you can. The timer starts on your first keystroke. As you type, each character is compared with the sample: matches count toward your speed and mismatches count as errors. When you reach the end of the sample, your final WPM and accuracy are frozen.

How WPM and accuracy are calculated

The standard convention counts one word as five keystrokes, including spaces. Gross WPM = (characters typed / 5) / minutes elapsed. Net WPM subtracts uncorrected errors: Net WPM = Gross WPM - (errors / minutes). Accuracy = correct characters / total typed x 100%. For Chinese, speed is often reported as characters per minute, because one Chinese character carries more information than a single Latin keystroke.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good typing speed?

Around 40 WPM is average for everyday computer users. 65-75 WPM is a common professional benchmark, and touch typists often reach 100 WPM or more. For Chinese pinyin input with prediction, people usually compare characters per minute rather than WPM.

What does WPM mean?

Words per minute. By convention one word equals five keystrokes including spaces, so WPM = (characters / 5) / minutes.

What is net WPM?

Net WPM is your gross WPM minus uncorrected errors per minute. It rewards accuracy, not just raw speed.

Is my typing uploaded anywhere?

No. The test runs entirely in your browser; the sample text and what you type never leave your device.

How can I type faster in Chinese?

Use touch typing for the pinyin keys, learn common phrase shortcuts, and let cloud prediction complete long words. The Sogou Input Method desktop client offers cloud input and phrase prediction.

Does the test support Chinese sample text?

Yes. Switch to the Chinese sample to practice Chinese typing, or switch to the English sample for Latin-script speed.
